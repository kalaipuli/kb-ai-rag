Title: Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) Setup Guide

Overview
Multi-Factor Authentication is mandatory for all corporate accounts. MFA adds a second layer of verification beyond your password, protecting accounts even if credentials are compromised. Microsoft Authenticator is the required MFA application for all employees.

Installing Microsoft Authenticator
Download Microsoft Authenticator from the Apple App Store (iOS) or Google Play Store (Android). Search for "Microsoft Authenticator" and install the official app published by Microsoft Corporation.

Registering Your Account
1. On your desktop, navigate to aka.ms/mysecurityinfo and sign in with your corporate credentials.
2. Click "Add sign-in method" and select "Authenticator app".
3. Click "Next" and then "Next" again on the "Set up your account" screen.
4. Open Microsoft Authenticator on your phone. Tap the "+" icon and select "Work or school account".
5. Select "Scan a QR code" and scan the QR code displayed on screen.
6. A 6-digit code will appear in the app. Enter it on screen to confirm registration.
7. Click "Next" and then "Done".

Authentication Methods Priority
Employees must register at least two methods. Recommended order:
1. Microsoft Authenticator push notification (most secure, works without cellular signal on Wi-Fi)
2. Microsoft Authenticator TOTP code (works offline)
3. SMS to mobile number (backup — less secure)
4. Phone call (emergency only)

Using MFA When Signing In
After entering your password, you will be prompted for MFA. If you have push notifications enabled, approve the notification on your phone. Alternatively, open the Authenticator app and enter the 6-digit code shown for your account.

Troubleshooting
- Push notification not arriving: Check mobile data/Wi-Fi is active. Open the Authenticator app and use the TOTP code instead.
- Lost or replaced phone: Contact the Help Desk immediately at extension 4357. They will temporarily disable MFA on your account after identity verification, allowing you to re-register a new device.
- Authenticator app out of sync: In the app, go to Settings > Time Correction for Codes > Sync Now.
- "Request denied" error: Do not approve MFA requests you did not initiate. Report unexpected prompts to security@company.internal.

Traveling Without Mobile Service
When traveling internationally, use the Authenticator app TOTP codes (work offline without any cellular or Wi-Fi connection). If traveling to a country where Microsoft services are blocked, contact IT at least 5 business days in advance.

Phishing Awareness
Never approve an MFA push notification if you are not actively signing in. Attackers use "MFA fatigue" attacks — repeatedly sending push requests hoping a user approves accidentally. If you receive unexpected MFA prompts, deny them and report to security@company.internal immediately.
