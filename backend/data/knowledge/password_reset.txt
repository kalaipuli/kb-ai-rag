Title: Password Reset Policy and Self-Service Guide

Overview
Corporate passwords are governed by the IT Security Password Policy (POL-SEC-002). All employees are required to change their Active Directory password every 90 days. Passwords that expire lock out access to email, VPN, SharePoint, and all Azure AD-integrated applications.

Password Requirements
- Minimum length: 14 characters
- Must include: at least one uppercase letter, one lowercase letter, one digit, and one special character (!@#$%^&*)
- Cannot reuse the last 24 passwords
- Cannot contain your username or display name
- Complexity check is enforced by Active Directory

Self-Service Password Reset
Employees can reset their own password without contacting the Help Desk by using the Self-Service Password Reset (SSPR) portal at password.company.internal.

Steps:
1. Navigate to password.company.internal.
2. Enter your corporate email address.
3. Verify your identity using one of the registered methods: Microsoft Authenticator push notification, SMS to registered mobile number, or security questions (if previously configured).
4. Enter and confirm your new password, meeting all complexity requirements.
5. After reset, allow 2–3 minutes for the change to propagate to all systems before attempting to log in.

SSPR Registration
All employees must register for SSPR within their first week. Navigate to aka.ms/mysecurityinfo and add at least two verification methods. Without registration you cannot use self-service reset and must contact the Help Desk.

Help Desk Assisted Reset
If you are locked out and cannot complete SSPR (e.g., lost phone, unregistered account), contact the Help Desk:
- Phone: extension 4357 (24/7)
- Email: helpdesk@company.internal
- Walk-in: IT Support Desk, Building A, Room 102 (Mon–Fri 8am–6pm)

Identity verification is required for Help Desk resets. Have your employee ID ready.

Password Manager
The company provides a licensed Bitwarden Teams account for all employees. Use it to generate and store strong unique passwords for SaaS applications. Contact helpdesk@company.internal with subject "Bitwarden access" to receive an invitation.

Important Notes
Do not share passwords with colleagues, including your manager. IT staff will never ask for your password. Report suspected credential compromise immediately to the Security team at security@company.internal.
